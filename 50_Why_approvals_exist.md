# Why Approvals Exist

## Overview

Approvals are a fundamental part of the Intain Markets platform. Understanding why approvals exist helps you understand the workflow and know what to expect at each stage.

## Purpose of Approvals

### Quality Assurance

**Why approvals ensure quality:**
- Items are reviewed before proceeding
- Standards are maintained
- Quality is verified
- Issues are caught early
- Only good items proceed

**Examples:**
- Term sheets reviewed before master commitment creation
- Funding requests reviewed before funding
- Pools reviewed before deal finalization

---

### Compliance

**Why approvals ensure compliance:**
- Regulatory requirements met
- Business rules followed
- Policies enforced
- Standards maintained
- Compliance verified

**Examples:**
- Facility rules compliance checked
- Borrowing base limits verified
- Documentation requirements met
- Eligibility criteria verified

---

### Risk Management

**Why approvals manage risk:**
- Risk is assessed before commitment
- Problems identified early
- Risk is controlled
- Decisions are informed
- Protection for all parties

**Examples:**
- Borrower creditworthiness evaluated
- Facility terms assessed
- Drawdown requests evaluated
- Risk profile reviewed

---

### Transparency

**Why approvals provide transparency:**
- Clear decision points
- Visible review process
- Documented decisions
- Audit trail maintained
- All parties informed

**Examples:**
- Review status visible to all
- Decisions documented
- Reasons provided
- History tracked

---

## Types of Approvals

### Single Approval

**What it means:**
- One party approves
- Workflow proceeds immediately
- Simple approval mechanism

**Examples:**
- Facility agent approves term sheet
- Lender approves master commitment
- Facility agent approves funding request

---

### Multi-Party Approval

**What it means:**
- Multiple parties must approve
- Can be sequential or parallel
- More complex approval process

**Examples:**
- Lenders approve master commitments (any one activates)
- Lenders approve funding notices (individual decisions)

---

### Sequential Approval

**What it means:**
- Approvals happen in order
- Each approval unlocks next step
- Workflow progresses step by step

**Examples:**
- Term sheet → Master commitment → Funding request
- Each step requires previous approval

---

## Approval Gates

### What Are Gates?

**Definition:**
- Checkpoints in workflow
- Must pass to proceed
- Ensure proper order
- Control workflow progression

**How they work:**
- Gate is closed (can't proceed)
- Approval opens gate
- Workflow continues
- Next gate awaits

---

### Why Gates Exist

**Purpose:**
- Ensure proper order
- Prevent skipping steps
- Maintain workflow integrity
- Protect all parties
- Ensure completeness

**Benefits:**
- Nothing missed
- Proper sequence
- Quality maintained
- Risk controlled

---

## Approval Workflow

### Standard Flow

**Typical pattern:**
1. Item created → Draft status
2. Item submitted → Review status
3. Reviewer evaluates → Decision made
4. Approved → Next phase
5. Or rejected → Stops
6. Or changes requested → Iteration

---

### Iterative Improvement

**Change requests enable:**
- Issues can be fixed
- Items can be improved
- Iterative refinement
- Better outcomes
- Quality improvement

**Process:**
- Changes requested
- Item edited
- Resubmitted
- Reviewed again
- Approved or more changes

---

## Benefits of Approvals

### For All Parties

**Protection:**
- All parties protected
- Quality maintained

- Transparency provided

**Confidence:**
- Decisions are informed

## Best Practices

1. **Understand purpose**: Know why approvals exist
2. **Prepare thoroughly**: Submit quality items
3. **Respond promptly**: Address change requests quickly
4. **Be patient**: Allow time for review
5. **Learn from feedback**: Improve based on decisions
