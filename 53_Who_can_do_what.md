# Who Can Do What

## Overview

This guide explains what each role can and cannot do in the Intain Markets platform. Understanding role permissions helps you know what actions are available to you.

## Who Can Use This

- All platform users

## When This Is Used

Understanding role permissions helps when:
- You want to know what actions you can take
- You need to understand what others can do
- You want to know why certain actions are unavailable
- You need to understand role-based access

## Step-by-Step Process

### Issuer (Borrower) Permissions

1. **Pools and Loans**
   - Can create pools
   - Can map loans to pools
   - Can edit pool information when status allows
   - Can share pools with other organizations
   - Can manage loan statuses

2. **Credit Facilities**
   - Can create term sheets
   - Can sign term sheets
   - Can submit term sheets for review
   - Can create funding requests
   - Can approve token transfers

3. **Cannot Do**
   - Cannot approve their own term sheets
   - Cannot create master commitments
   - Cannot approve their own funding requests
   - Cannot sign funding notices

### Market Maker / Facility Agent Permissions

1. **Pools**
   - Can review pools shared with them
   - Can accept or reject pool mandates
   - Can request changes to pools
   - Can structure deals
   - Can finalize pools as deals

2. **Credit Facilities**
   - Can review term sheets
   - Can approve or reject term sheets
   - Can request changes to term sheets
   - Can configure master commitments
   - Can review funding requests
   - Can generate tokens
   - Can sign funding notices for lenders

3. **Cannot Do**
   - Cannot create pools
   - Cannot create term sheets
   - Cannot approve master commitments as lenders
   - Cannot create funding requests

### Investor / Lender Permissions

1. **Pools**
   - Can review pool previews
   - Can analyze investment opportunities
   - Can express interest
   - Can provide feedback

2. **Credit Facilities**
   - Can approve master commitments
   - Can review funding notices
   - Can approve or reject drawdowns
   - Can confirm fund transfers

3. **Cannot Do**
   - Cannot create pools
   - Cannot create term sheets
   - Cannot create master commitments
   - Cannot create funding requests

## Rules & Validations

- Each role has specific permissions
- You can only perform actions allowed by your role
- Some actions require specific roles
- Permissions are enforced by the system
- Role determines what you can see and do

## What Happens Next

After understanding role permissions:
- You know what actions are available to you
- You understand what others can do
- You know why certain actions are unavailable
- You can work effectively within your role
- You understand collaboration between roles

Understanding role permissions helps you know what you can do and how to work effectively within your role's capabilities.
