# What Happens After Rejection

## Overview

When an item is rejected, it enters a final state with specific consequences. Understanding what happens after rejection helps you know what to expect and how to proceed.

## Who Can Use This

- Borrowers who receive rejections
- Issuers who receive rejections
- All users who want to understand rejection outcomes

## When This Is Used

Understanding rejection outcomes helps when:
- Your submission has been rejected
- You want to know what to do next
- You need to understand why you can't edit rejected items
- You want to learn from rejections

## Step-by-Step Process

### Understanding Rejection

1. **Rejection Means Final State**
   - Item is rejected and process stops
   - No further action possible on rejected item
   - Cannot be edited or resubmitted
   - Must create new item to try again

2. **What You Cannot Do**
   - Cannot edit rejected item
   - Cannot resubmit same item
   - Cannot appeal rejection
   - Cannot change status

3. **What You Can Do**
   - View rejected item
   - See rejection reason if provided
   - Create new item
   - Learn from rejection
   - Try again with improvements

### After Term Sheet Rejection

1. **What Happens**
   - Term sheet status shows as rejected
   - No master commitment is created
   - Facility does not proceed
   - You receive notification

2. **Next Steps**
   - Review rejection reason
   - Address identified issues
   - Create new term sheet
   - Try again with improvements

### After Funding Request Rejection

1. **What Happens**
   - Funding request status shows as rejected
   - No funding notice is created
   - Drawdown does not proceed
   - You receive notification

2. **Next Steps**
   - Review rejection reason
   - Address issues identified
   - Create new funding request
   - Adjust amount if capacity was issue

### After Pool Mandate Rejection

1. **What Happens**
   - Market maker rejects mandate
   - Pool may return to Preview status
   - Rejection is recorded
   - You receive notification

2. **Next Steps**
   - Review rejection feedback
   - Address concerns if possible
   - Share with other market makers
   - Continue pool preparation

## Rules & Validations

- Rejected items cannot be edited or resubmitted
- You must create new items to try again
- Rejection reasons help you understand what to fix
- Rejections are final decisions
- You can learn from rejections and improve

## What Happens Next

After understanding rejection outcomes:
- You know what to expect when items are rejected
- You understand why you can't edit rejected items
- You know you must create new items to try again
- You can learn from rejection reasons
- You can improve future submissions

Understanding what happens after rejection helps you know how to proceed and improve your future submissions.
