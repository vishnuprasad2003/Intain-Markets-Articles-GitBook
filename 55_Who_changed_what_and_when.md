# Who Changed What and When

## Overview

The platform tracks every change with complete details about who made the change, what changed, and when it happened. This guide explains how to view and understand this change history, helping you track item progression, maintain accountability, and understand the complete history of any item.

## What This Means for the User

When you view items in the platform, you can see:

- **Complete Change History**: Every change is recorded with who made it and when.

- **Status Progression**: You can see how items progressed through their workflows.

- **Action Tracking**: All actions are logged, showing what was done and by whom.

- **Document History**: Document uploads and changes are tracked.

- **Accountability**: Every change is attributed to specific users.

- **Transparency**: Complete visibility into all changes and activities.

This helps you understand how items reached their current state and who was responsible for each change.

## Key Concepts Explained

### Status History

Status history shows all status changes:

- **Status Changes**: Every time an item's status changes, it's recorded.

- **Who Changed It**: The user who changed the status is recorded.

- **When It Changed**: Precise timestamp shows when the change occurred.

- **Previous Status**: The status before the change is recorded.

- **New Status**: The new status after the change is recorded.

- **Reason or Comments**: Reasons or comments explaining the change may be included.

- **Chronological Order**: All status changes are shown in chronological order.

### Action History

Action history shows all actions taken:

- **Action Type**: What action was taken (create, update, approve, reject, etc.).

- **Who Took Action**: Which user performed the action.

- **When Action Occurred**: Timestamp showing when the action happened.

- **What Was Affected**: Which item or items were affected.

- **Result**: What happened as a result of the action.

- **Details**: Additional details about the action may be included.

- **Chronological Order**: All actions are shown in chronological order.

### Document History

Document history tracks document-related activities:

- **Document Uploads**: When documents were uploaded and by whom.

- **Document Changes**: Any modifications to documents are recorded.

- **Previous Versions**: Previous versions of documents are saved.

- **Version History**: Complete version history is maintained.

- **Access Tracking**: Who accessed documents and when may be tracked.

### Viewing Change History

You can view change history by:

- **Status History Section**: Navigate to item details and find Status History section.

- **Action History Section**: Navigate to item details and find Action History section.

- **Activity Log**: Combined view showing all activities chronologically.

- **Audit Trail**: Complete audit trail showing all changes and actions.

- **Document History**: Document section showing upload and change history.

## Important Points to Know

- All changes are tracked automatically - tracking happens automatically - you don't need to do anything special.

- History cannot be modified or deleted - audit trails are permanent and cannot be altered.

- Complete information is recorded - every change includes who, what, and when.

- Timestamps are accurate - all timestamps use consistent time standards.

- User information is included for all changes - every change is attributed to specific users.

- Status history shows progression - you can see how items progressed through workflows.

- Action history shows what happened - you can see all actions taken on items.

- Document history maintains versions - previous versions are saved for reference.

- Complete transparency - all relevant parties can see relevant history.

- Easy to access - history is easily accessible from item details pages.

Understanding who changed what and when helps you track item history, maintain accountability, understand process flow, review audit information, and see the complete progression of any item from creation to completion.
