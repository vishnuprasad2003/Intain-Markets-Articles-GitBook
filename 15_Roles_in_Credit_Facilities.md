# Roles in Credit Facilities

## Overview

Credit facilities involve three main parties, each with distinct responsibilities and permissions. Understanding these roles helps you know what you can do and what's expected of you.

## Who Can Use This

- Borrowers who create and use credit facilities
- Facility Agents who structure and manage facilities
- Lenders who provide capital

## When This Is Used

Understanding roles helps when:
- You need to know what actions you can take
- You want to understand what others can do
- You need to know who to contact for different tasks
- You want to understand the workflow

## Step-by-Step Process

### Borrower Responsibilities

1. **Create Term Sheets**
   - Create term sheets with facility terms
   - Sign via electronic signature
   - Submit for facility agent review
   - Respond to change requests

2. **Create Funding Requests**
   - Create funding requests against active facilities
   - Specify amounts and purposes
   - Submit for review
   - Approve token transfers

### Facility Agent Responsibilities

1. **Review Term Sheets**
   - Review term sheets submitted by borrowers
   - Approve or request changes
   - Configure master commitments after approval

2. **Manage Facilities**
   - Set up facility rules and parameters
   - Configure lender groups
   - Review funding requests
   - Sign funding notices for lenders

### Lender Responsibilities

1. **Approve Facilities**
   - Review master commitments
   - Approve facilities via electronic signature
   - Any lender approval activates the facility

2. **Review Drawdowns**
   - Review funding notices
   - Approve or reject individual drawdowns
   - Confirm fund transfers

## Rules & Validations

- Borrowers cannot approve their own term sheets or funding requests
- Facility agents cannot create term sheets or funding requests
- Lenders cannot create facilities but can approve them
- Each role has specific permissions and cannot perform actions reserved for other roles

## What Happens Next

After understanding roles:
- You know what actions you can take
- You understand what others can do
- You know who to collaborate with
- You can work effectively within your role's responsibilities

Understanding roles in credit facilities helps you know what you can do, what's expected of you, and how to effectively participate in the credit facility workflow.
